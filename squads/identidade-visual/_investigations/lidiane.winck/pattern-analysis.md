Her core brand strength is positioning herself as a translator between clinical knowledge and everyday parental life.

## Language Patterns

### Tone Profile
Lidiane writes as an expert who has decided to stop being distant. The tone is authoritative but non-condescending — she uses colloquial markers ("viu?", "hein?", "né") inside technical content, creating a hybrid register that signals both expertise and accessibility. She writes for parents who are overwhelmed, not for professionals who want to impress peers. There is a consistent undercurrent of validating maternal experience — acknowledging exhaustion, guilt, anxiety — before redirecting to professional guidance.

### Hook Patterns

1. **"Comparar parece inofensivo… Mas, especialmente no desenvolvimento infantil, é uma armadilha, viu? 👀"**
   Pattern: False sense of security + pivot to danger + colloquial tag ("viu?") + emoji as punctuation

2. **"Existe um provérbio africano que diz: 'é preciso uma aldeia para educar uma criança.' 🤝"**
   Pattern: Cultural authority reference → opens with external wisdom, not personal opinion

3. **"Observar não é rotular! Esses sinais não significam, automaticamente, diagnóstico de TEA — mas indicam que a criança precisa de avaliação qualificada. 👉"**
   Pattern: Permission statement (reframes a fear/judgment) + redirect to positive action + directional emoji

4. **"Já que o autismo não é receita de bolo, os níveis de suporte servem para responder uma pergunta prática: quanto suporte essa pessoa precisa no dia a dia? 🤔"**
   Pattern: Analogy/metaphor ("receita de bolo") → practical reframe → direct question

5. **"A partir de agora, quero dividir mais sobre o lado de cá: o cotidiano, os aprendizados, o que dá certo (e o que nem tanto)..."**
   Pattern: Intimate declaration of intent + vulnerability signal ("o que nem tanto") + ellipsis creating anticipation

### Call-to-Action Patterns

1. **Question CTA (most common):** "Vamos falar sobre isso: você já se sentiu sozinha na maternidade?" — invites comment/conversation. Used in 3 of 9 posts.
2. **Directional emoji CTA:** "👉" at end of opening sentence — signals "swipe/continue reading." Used in carousel posts.
3. **Implicit follow CTA:** No explicit follow request — content quality is the CTA. Hashtag block closes the post.
4. **Gratitude/acknowledgment close:** In professional event posts, closes with extended thanks that restate values ("ninguém trabalha sozinho"). No direct CTA — authority speak.

### Vocabulary Signature
- **"avaliação funcional / avaliar funcionalmente"** — appears in multiple posts; frames the role of the TO as practical problem-solver, not diagnostician
- **"plasticidade cerebral"** — technical term used without apology; her audience is assumed capable of learning it
- **"impacto funcional"** — another TO-specific term used fluently in parent-facing content
- **"regulação sensorial / processamento sensorial"** — core conceptual pillars of her specialty
- **"espectro de funcionamento"** — reframes autism in functional rather than hierarchical terms
- **"rede"** (rede de cuidado, rede ausente) — recurring metaphor for support systems
- **"ritmo possível"** — gentle, non-pressuring framing of child development pace
- Colloquials: **"viu?"**, **"hein?"**, **"né"** — appear at end of technical sentences to soften register

### Style Notes
- **Sentence length:** Mixed — short declarative punches alternating with longer explanatory sentences. Never more than 2–3 sentences without a break.
- **Emoji usage:** Functional, not decorative. Used as: sentence-ending punctuation (😉), directional signal (👉), opening hook emphasis (👀), warmth closer (🥰, 💙). Never more than 1–2 per paragraph.
- **Formatting:** Frequent line breaks. Bullet points using 👉 as marker. All caps: not used. Bold: not used in captions. Hashtag block always appears as a separate final section.
- **Hashtag strategy:** Small and consistent (3–6 tags). Always #LidianeWinck (branded). Then 2 topical tags per post. Never more than 6. No mass hashtag strategies.
- **Rhetorical figures:** Uses antithesis frequently ("não é X, é Y" structure) to clarify. E.g., "Autismo não tem escala de 'mais ou menos'. Tem espectro de funcionamento!" — strong contrast for clarity.

For Clínica Base, this approach means picking ONE strong sentence per slide and letting the typography carry it. The B.A.S.E. method has conceptual depth — give each core concept its own slide with editorial typographic treatment.

3. **Establish a consistent vocabulary that bridges clinical and parental language — and make it visual.** @lidiane.winck's signature vocabulary ("avaliação funcional", "impacto funcional", "ritmo possível") creates a recognizable intellectual framework. For Clínica Base, the B.A.S.E. method already provides this: Brincar, Afeto, Sensorial, Estimulação are the branded pillars. Each pillar could have its own visual treatment — consistent color variant, typography style — creating a system where content is instantly recognizable as Clínica Base even without reading the name.

4. **Build authority through personal and professional milestone content — not only educational posts.** @lidiane.winck's highest-engagement posts are personal (188 likes) and professional event (82 likes), not educational (25–48 likes). For Clínica Base, Regiane's presence as a named professional (CREFITO-3 11824-TO) with a story, partnerships, and milestone moments should be front and center. Content showing Regiane at events, training, or working (with appropriate patient privacy) will outperform pure infographic posts.

5. **Apply the "permission statement" hook pattern to Clínica Base content.** The most effective hook pattern observed is the reframe: "Observar não é rotular" defuses parental guilt before delivering clinical information. This hook structure immediately validates the parent's experience, reduces defensiveness, and opens them to the professional guidance that follows. It should become a structural template for all educational content.
